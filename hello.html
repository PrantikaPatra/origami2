Interesting facts about Camels

* Camels can completely close their nostrils during sandstorms

* Camels can drink 50 gallons of water in one go

* Camels can travel upto 40 miles per hour

* Mother camels carry their calves up to 14 months before giving birth

Interesting facts about Chameleons

* Their feet work like salad tongs

* Chameleons vary wildly in terms of size

* Chameleons cannot grow their tail

* Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads

Interesting facts about Pigeons

* Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals

* They can find their way back to the nest from 1300 miles away.

* You can train them to be art snobs

* Pigeons produce fake "milk"

Interesting facts about TeddyBears

* A teddy has been into space! Magellan T Bear boarded Space Shuttle Discovery in 1995 as part of a school project.

* The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.

* Meanwhile, in Germany in the same year, the Steiff family developed a silk-like fabric bear, based on zoo animals. They caused a sensation at a toy fair and were snapped up by an American buyer.

* In 1902 in Germany, Steiff launched “Bear 55 PB”, the first toy bear with jointed arms and legs.

Interesting facts about Pandas

* Their eyes are different to normal bears

* At 5 months old, giant pandas learn how to climb - sometimes practicing by climbing on their mum

* On average, pandas poo 40 times a day

* Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo

Interesting facts about FlyingCicada

* Male cicadas produce very loud calls that can damage human hearing.

* Cicadas spend most of their lives underground

* Cicadas make noise like toy frogs

* Cicadas are like tiny violins
